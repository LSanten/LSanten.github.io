Welcome!


I recently graduated with a concentration in robotics from Olin College of Engineering near Boston (May 2021). Before my college time, I grew up in Frankfurt, Germany. I'm currently working on building fish-safe and eco-system friendly water turbines at Natel Energy.
I'm interested in exploring the role of engineering in our worldwide challenges ranging from climate change to systemic injustice. Some of my exciting engineering work ranges from building and maintaining renewable energy systems such as a vertical wind turbine, micro-hydro generators to designing and constructing a sun-tracker (pan-tilt mechanism) for five solar panels. I also love to express my engineering skills in artistic, playful, and beauty-creating ways - just like nature :-)

In the academic world, I published a paper with the Technical University of Munich on humanized driving assistance for increased road collaboration. At Olin College, I ran a study on the effects of behavior prompts on laundry habits to minimze energy and water consumption and create a social awareness around our habits. At Natel Energy, I conducted controlled laboratory tests to investigate the effect of downstream passage through water turbines on eels. I contributed to large parts of the post-processing and data analysis. Soon, you will find our study in a peer-reviewed journal.

My understanding of the interconnectedness in nature and my interest in sustainable engineering has grown a lot during a two-semester immersion at an off-grid permaculture farm in North Carolina, which I helped to initiate and organize (olinatwoodlandharvest.com). During the first semester, 15 students came together with the farm family to live in community, be close to nature, and be creative. During this time, I wrote my Bachelor's thesis on Holistic engineering and a science of holism for a thriving world. In a transdisciplinary fashion, I believe that engineering can do much better in working with living systems and the organism we live on - Earth. Frankly, engineers have created almost all technology that has contributed to the destruction of our planet. I am curious to understand more about how oppression, violence, and colonialist power structures are weaved into this field of great creative potential; especially as a white, privileged human in this unjust world.
