Welcome!


I am an engineering student with a concentration in robotics from Olin College of Engineering near Boston. I grew up and went to high school in Frankfurt, Germany. I am particularly interested in complex systems thinking and the role of engineering in our worldwide challenges ranging from climate change to systemic injustice. I built and maintained renewable energy systems such as a wind turbine, micro-hydro generators, and solar panels. I also love to tinker with sustainable design and artistic engineering projects.

In the academic world, I published a paper with the Technical University of Munich on humanized driving assistance for increased road collaboration. At Olin College, I ran a study on the effects of behavior prompts on laundry habits to minimze energy and water consumption and create a social awareness around our habits.

During my last year of college (and the COVID pandemic), I spent my time at an off-grid permaculture farm with many other students. During this time, I wrote my Bachelor's thesis on Holistic engineering and a science of holism for a thriving world. In a transdisciplinary fashion, I believe that engineering can do much better in working with living systems and the organism we live on - Earth.
